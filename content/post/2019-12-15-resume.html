Introduction

This project explores the interaction of several different variables related to the ongoing opioid crisis in the United States. There were four different data sets combined. The data sets for opioid prescription rates per 100 people and drug ovdeaths per 100,000 people on a county level in 2016 were taken from the CDC. The data sets for median household income on a county level and percentage of adults lacking a high school diploma on a county level in 2016 were taken from the US Census Bureau. The expected association is that low income counties will have high levels of opioid prescription rates, drug overdose rates, and percentages of people without higschool diplomas.
