Python has many powerful bioinformatics tools. One important task in bioinformatics is finding restriction binding sites in genes. Restriction enzymes are proteins that bind the DNA sequences and cleave them at specific sites. DNA consists of four bases adenine, guanine, cytosine, and thiamine designated by A,G,C, and T in the sequence respectively. If a certain restriction enzyme matched at ANTAAT to GCRWTG, then searching a long sequence for those specific sequences would be an important procedure. (N can be any base, R is A or G, and W is A or T) In python, regular expressions can make this task very easy. Regular expressions offer a way to match characters in a string to a desired string and return the matched strings.
